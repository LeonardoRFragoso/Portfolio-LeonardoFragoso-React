# International Positioning Report — Portfolio Leonardo Fragoso

**Date:** 2026-06-25  
**Goal:** Make the portfolio attractive to US, UK and EU recruiters.

---

## 1. Current State

### 1.1 Language

- The portfolio has i18n (PT/EN) and the toggle works.
- Most copy is translated.
- Some UI elements may still be hardcoded in Portuguese.

### 1.2 Tone and Positioning

- Current PT copy: "Busco uma equipe onde possa crescer" — junior-adjacent.
- Current EN copy: "Looking to join a team where I can contribute, learn and keep growing" — growth-oriented, but could sound junior to US recruiters.

### 1.3 Technical Terminology

- PT uses "Sistemas Enterprise" and "IA Aplicada".
- EN uses "Corporate Systems" and "Applied AI".
- Good, but could be more aligned with US job titles: "Enterprise Systems", "Backend Engineering", "API Architecture", "AI Engineering".

### 1.4 URLs and SEO

- Domain is `.vercel.app` — acceptable, but a custom domain (e.g., `leonardofragoso.dev`) would look more professional.
- Meta tags are in English but `og:locale` is `pt_BR`.

### 1.5 Visual Identity

- Dark theme with gradient accents is modern and globally acceptable.
- Photo is professional.
- No obvious cultural barriers.

---

## 2. Issues Found

### 2.1 English Copy Still Sounds Like Translation

Some phrases are literal translations from Portuguese:
- "Backend, AI and systems that deliver results" — okay, but could be sharper.
- "Not just code — results in production" — good, but could be more punchy.

### 2.2 Hero Description Emphasizes Growth

"Looking to join a team where I can contribute, learn and keep growing" — while honest, it signals "I am not senior yet" to US recruiters.

### 2.3 Some Content Remains Portuguese-Only

- Some footer links and internal labels may still be hardcoded.
- The CV download link uses Portuguese filenames.

### 2.4 Job Title

- "Python Backend Developer" is clear and honest.
- For US market, "Backend Engineer" or "Software Engineer — Backend" might match job titles better.

### 2.5 Contact Section

- WhatsApp is Brazil-centric. US recruiters may prefer email or LinkedIn.
- Email should be more prominent for international outreach.

---

## 3. Recommendations

### 3.1 English Copy Refresh

**Hero (current):**
> Python Backend Developer. I build APIs, Applied AI and Corporate Systems for companies that need to scale.

**Recommended:**
> Backend Engineer | Python · FastAPI · Django. I design APIs, AI systems and enterprise platforms that scale.

**Why Hire Me (current):**
> Backend, AI and systems that deliver results.

**Recommended:**
> Backend Engineering · AI Systems · Production SaaS.

### 3.2 Reframe Growth Narrative

Instead of "looking to learn and grow", use:
> "I build systems used in production. My focus is backend engineering, API design and applied AI. I am looking for a team where I can solve complex problems and ship high-impact systems."

### 3.3 Internationalize Contact

- Keep WhatsApp but make Email/LinkedIn more prominent for international recruiters.
- Add a Calendly link visible for US/EU time zones.
- Consider adding a "Schedule a call" button.

### 3.4 Custom Domain

- Register `leonardofragoso.dev` or `leonardofragoso.com`.
- Configure Vercel custom domain.
- Update LinkedIn and CV to use the custom domain.

### 3.5 CV in English

- Ensure the English CV is already available and matches the portfolio title.
- Add a "Download CV (English)" button if the user is in English mode.

### 3.6 Case Studies in English

- When creating case studies, write them first in English, then translate to Portuguese.
- English-first content reads more natural to international recruiters.

### 3.7 Social Proof Reframe

- "Brazil's largest private port terminal" is great for Brazilian context.
- For international context: "Enterprise systems deployed at a major global port terminal operator."
